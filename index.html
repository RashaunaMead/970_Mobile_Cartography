      About This Project

      Madison's East Isthmus: Paths to Change mobile learning module was created for the Introduction to International Studies (IS101) course at the University of Wisconsin-Madison by Spring 2014 Mobile Cartography Seminar participants Brian Davidson, Julia Janicki, Vanessa Knoppe-Wetzel, Fei Ma, Rashauna Mead, Chelsea Nestel, Caroline Rose, Carl Sack, and Guiming Zhang under the direction of Professor Robert Roth, with the cooperation of International Studies Professor Stephen Young and Teaching Assistant Mario Bruzzone. Narration was written and performed by Stephen Young. Questions or suggestions about the project should be directed to Stephen Young (sjyoung3@wisc.edu) and Rob Roth (reroth@wisc.edu).

      Tour narration

      When you enter the map, you are presented with text box containing a brief overview of the global history of Madison, WI. Each landmark on the guided tour has a similar background description providing important context for understanding the place and responding to the slideshow prompts. Click Read Text Aloud to listen to an audio recording of the background description. If there are next > and < previous buttons in the window, click these to advance or review the text.

      Navigating to landmarks

      Follow the red route line to navigate to the next landmark. To see your current location on a mobile device, tap the icon (not visible on desktop view). This will activate a blue GPS dot at your location the map. To save your battery, the GPS dot will disappear after ten seconds, but you may reactivate it at any time. Pay attention to your surroundings and use caution in locations marked with the icon, which you may click or tap to see a description of the hazard. Your next destination is marked with a square red icon, with prior landmarks deactivated in gray. You can reactivate the route to a prior landmark by choosing that landmark in the Landmarks menu in the menu bar. Only landmarks you visited previously will appear in the Landmarks menu. To register your visit to a landmark, once you get there, click or tap the landmark's icon. This will open that landmark's slideshow prompts.

      Slideshow prompts

      When you reach a new landmark, click on the red square icon to activate the slideshow with images and prompts for the location. For each landmark, there are three thematic slides: Interdependencies, Inequalities, and Alternatives. Each prompt includes a pair of images comparing the landmark 'then' and 'now.' Use the slider between the images to compare the pair of images by swiping the slider back and forth. Use the white arrow buttons to change the slide. As you think about each prompt, take notes and photographs to support your visual essay. The final slide for each landmark is a button allowing you to Proceed to the Next Site, which then maps your route to the next location on the guided tour.

      Lost?

      On a mobile device, you can always see your location by clicking the icon. If at any time you get disoriented navigating the Isthmus, click on one of the following links to navigate to the landmark using Google Maps:

      • Landmark #1: Badger State Shoe Factory
      • Landmark #2: Chicago and Northwestern Railroad Station
      • Landmark #3: Power Plant Transformers
      • Landmark #4: Wil-Mar Community Center
      • Landmark #5: Ground Zero Cafe
      • UW Memorial Union

      Report a problem

      If you notice a bug or other issue, please e-mail a brief description to reroth@wisc.edu.

      Assignment

      All IS101 students: Once you’ve completed the walking tour, you need to arrange the notes and images you collected into a photo essay that explains how and why Madison’s East Isthmus has been transformed through its interconnections with other places. Please see the course site for further details.
